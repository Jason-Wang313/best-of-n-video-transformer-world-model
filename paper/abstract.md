# Abstract

Best-of-N selection over generated video futures can reward the most persuasive visual tail rather than the most useful action-conditioned future. We study this effect in a controlled low-resolution world with rendered counterfactuals. As `N` increases, selected futures can become smoother and more goal-like while the same actions execute into blocked or occluded dynamics. We provide a tie-aware finite-pool law, video-specific diagnostics, and a repair ladder based on action consistency, temporal filtering, occlusion uncertainty, frame-state agreement, and small held-out calibration. The result is a scoped counterexample and audit protocol for video future selection.
