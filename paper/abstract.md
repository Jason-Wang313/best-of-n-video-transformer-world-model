# Abstract

Top-score selection over generated video futures can reward the most persuasive visual tail rather than the most useful future. We study this effect in a controlled action-conditioned world with rendered counterfactuals and a compact Moving-MNIST future-prediction tier. As candidate count increases, selected futures can become smoother and more goal-like while the same actions execute into blocked or occluded dynamics, or while standard bouncing-digit futures drift toward visually smooth but inaccurate motion. We provide a tie-aware finite-pool law, video-specific diagnostics, benchmark baselines, and a repair ladder based on action consistency, temporal filtering, occlusion uncertainty, frame-state agreement, motion support, and small held-out calibration. The result is a scoped counterexample and audit protocol for video future selection.
